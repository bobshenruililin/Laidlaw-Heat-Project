# Canon: people, contributions and meeting norms

**Purpose:** preserve who contributes what so technical work does not erase ownership, credit or the human decisions the project still needs. These are project roles as understood on 23 July 2026, not job titles or permanent boundaries.

## Hogan

**Contributions**

- Leads weather and heat framing for this project.
- Brought the Goggins studies in as the historical foundation and comparability challenge.
- Proposed keeping ages 65–69 and 70–74 visible where governed data permit.
- Contributed the climate \(X\) layer and pointed the team to Li et al., *Atmospheric Research* (`10.1016/j.atmosres.2024.107845`).
- Proposed the project adaptation: define Li heatwaves, count them by month, then flag upper-tail event-count months.
- Has offered Bob guidance on academic writing: plainer, less flowery and easier to digest.

**Meeting norm**

Begin by asking how Hogan frames the weather question now. Credit his count-to-tail adaptation explicitly, present real choices rather than a finished answer, and read back the written weather decisions for correction. Weather definitions are locked **with** Hogan, not handed to him for approval after the fact.

**Contributions**

- Leads the governed Hospital Authority stroke aggregates, dictionary/transfer questions and outcome timing.

Do not infer schema, event timing, suppressed cells or transfer permissions. Ask Roro for the field-level rule and final source manifest, keep mortality and stroke estimands separate, and send concise written records for correction. The revised manuscript, not a silent interpretation of medRxiv v1, should settle ambiguous `HWD_*` operators.

## Shared norms

1. Name the lead before discussing the artifact: Hogan for weather; Roro for outcome timing and governed aggregates; Bishai for PI/concept decisions; Bob for reproducible implementation and record-keeping.
2. Ask before closing a human-owned choice.
3. Separate `locked`, `proposed` and `pending` decisions in writing.
4. Distinguish daily mortality AFs/RRs, modeled excess deaths and monthly stroke associations.
5. Credit ideas at the level they occurred: source event rule, project adaptation, outcome rule, model implementation and writing.
6. Finish consequential meetings with a read-back and a short written record that invites correction.
