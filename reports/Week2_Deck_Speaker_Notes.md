# Week 2 Progress — Bob’s point-based speaking notes

## Cheat sheet — hardest jargon

| Term | Plain-English meaning |
|---|---|
| **Goggins baseline challenge** | Earlier Hong Kong studies linked cold with stroke/heart-attack admissions; 2013–2023 is a comparison, not a first discovery. |
| **Stroke aggregates** | Monthly grouped stroke counts, possibly by age/sex—not patient records. |
| **Spells** | Consecutive days or nights meeting a temperature rule—not isolated extremes. |
| **2D3N** | Two very hot days plus three hot nights: sustained day-and-night heat. |
| **GOPC marker** | Clinic stroke mention pointing to an earlier hospitalised event—not automatically the event date. |
| **Hot-month options** | Pre-declared monthly labels using percentiles, official extremes, spells, or event counts. |
| **Align with Ren/Wang-style spells** | Reuse comparable Hong Kong day/night runs, connecting monthly and daily work. |
| **Daily DLNM** | Daily curved temperature–risk and delayed-effect model; monthly data cannot recover acute patterns. |
| **Hogan–Atmospheric Research recipe** | Define heatwaves, count monthly starts, then flag upper-tail count months. DOI `10.1016/j.atmosres.2024.107845`. |
| **Labelled panel—not seventeen discoveries** | Named robustness models: one frozen headline pair, with the rest labelled sensitivities. |
| **CHP flu** | Centre for Health Protection influenza surveillance; cold and flu seasons overlap. |

## If challenged — mini FAQ

- **Why monthly data?** They are the governed outcome and complement daily DLNM work.
- **Why not choose the best-fitting heat rule?** Model-shopping; freeze headlines and label sensitivities.
- **Are 14/7/5/39/38 stroke findings?** No—exposure counts across 132 months.
- **Why flu?** Cold and flu coincide; adjustment helps separate their signals.
- **Do climate/pollution trends explain stroke?** No—trends are not health associations.
- **Is Wang/Ren Jasmine’s paper?** Unknown; Dr Bishai’s source is the stop/go evidence.
- **Are we replicating?** Only with closely matched designs; otherwise this is a definition-harmonised extension.
