## Ask whether the Goggins evolutionary framing still holds

- “You first brought Goggins in as both the foundation and the challenge. Do you still see this as a later-period, evolutionary comparison—different heat extremes, population age structure and pollutant mix—or would you frame it differently now?”

- “Should ‘evolutionary’ stay as a broad time-change framing, or is that word doing too much?”

Let Hogan define the intellectual frame. Do not turn “evolutionary” into a biological, genetic or proven regime-shift claim.

## Walk the HM/CM starters, lightly

Start with the question, not the codes: “Could we choose the hot and cold pair to take into Gate 3, then keep the others as named sensitivities?”

Hot starters:

- `HM23`: Hogan’s Li-HW monthly event-count upper tail;
- `HM08`: monthly mean temperature at or above a frozen p90;
- `HM15`: month touches a run of at least five very hot days (`Tmax ≥33°C`);
- `HM17`: month touches a run of at least five hot nights (`Tmin ≥28°C`);
- `HM19`: month touches a Wang/Ren-style 2D3N window.

Cold starters:

- `CM03`: at least five days with `Tmin ≤12°C`;
- `CM08`: monthly mean temperature at or below a frozen p05;
- `CM15`: month touches at least three consecutive days with `Tmin ≤12°C`.

If useful after the core choice, mention `HM27` nighttime heat intensity, `HM32` heat plus ozone, `CM05` any day at or below 10°C, and `CM30` cold plus influenza. These are first-wave sensitivities, not extra co-primaries.

Useful prompts:

- “Which pair has the clearest scientific meaning before we see stroke outcomes?”
- “Would you rather lead with an event definition or a simple monthly percentile?”
- “Is there a starter here you would drop rather than carry forward?”

## Walk Roro’s four `HWD_*` definitions as siblings, not replacements

- “Roro’s paper gives us four distinct heatwave scenarios: `HWD_Tavg`, `HWD_Tmax`, `HWD_Tmin`, and `HWD_Tcombined`.”
- “Could these sit as four named monthly exposure siblings beside the HKO, Wang/Ren and Hogan families—not replace them?”
- “Their source mortality RRs would stay in the mortality paper. We would transport only fully specified exposure rules and estimate any stroke association from our own data.”

The exact monthly operators must wait for the final Roro source manifest because medRxiv v1 has wording ambiguities. Ask Hogan whether the family belongs in the Gate 3 panel; do not silently resolve the ambiguities in the room.

## Use Jasmine’s cold ≫ heat AF carefully

- “Jasmine is now locked as Jingwen Liu et al. 2020. The reported mortality AF was 4.72% for cold and 0.16% for heat; moderate-temperature AF was 4.25% versus 0.63% for extreme temperatures.”
- “Does that support treating cold as a genuine co-primary here, rather than a heat sensitivity?”
- “How much should that mortality result shape our exposure choices for a monthly stroke study?”

The useful point is symmetry and burden, not coefficient transport. The exact MMT and moderate/extreme cutoffs still require the full paper and supplement.

## What not to overclaim

- No stroke coefficient or project health finding exists yet; Tuesday’s graphs are exposure-only checks.
- Jasmine’s AFs are daily mortality results, not stroke effects.
- Wang/Ren and Roro mortality RRs are not coefficients for monthly stroke.
- Monthly associations cannot reproduce Goggins’ daily lag estimates or prove that risk has “shifted.”
- `CM05` at about 10°C is a severe-cold sensitivity, not a biological threshold.
- This ecological project cannot identify genes, ancestry effects or causal adaptation. Housing, cooling, behaviour, occupation, care, age structure and acclimatisation remain alternatives.
- Do not call Roro’s four definitions interchangeable, and do not choose among them by whichever later gives the largest estimate.
- Do not promise subtype, age-band or equity analyses until the governed aggregate schema and disclosure rules support them.

Try to leave with:

3. a written role for Roro’s four `HWD_*` siblings;
4. the threshold reference period and percentile/tie method;
5. `CM05` explicitly labelled as a sensitivity; and
6. the status of ages 65–69 and 70–74, subject to Roro’s governed schema.

For anything unresolved, write an owner and date. Close warmly: thank Hogan for the weather lead and for helping make the project—and Bob’s writing—clearer.
