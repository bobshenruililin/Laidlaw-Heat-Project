Why climate extremes matter for human health},before skip=0pt,after skip=1.5mm,width=\linewidth]
\RaggedRight\fontsize{9.8}{11.6}\selectfont

Climate change reshapes thermal exposure in subtropical cities. Overnight heat
impairs physiological recovery; cold snaps stress blood pressure, viscosity, and
autonomic tone---raising acute cardiac and cerebrovascular risk among older adults
in dense housing with limited overnight cooling.
\begin{itemize}
  \item Official HKO metrics drive public heat/cold warnings and preparedness
  \item Ages 65--74 nearly doubled (2013--2023), multiplying vulnerable person-time
  \item Hospital burden---not only mortality---matters for adaptation planning
\end{itemize}

\end{tcolorbox}

\textbf{Cold-dominant CVD (daily).}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Goggins 2013} (AMI 2000--09): below $\sim$24$^\circ$C,
        $\sim$\textbf{+3.7\% AMI / $^\circ$C} cooling (lags 0--13); \textbf{heat NS}; NO$_2$ key.
  \item \textbf{Goggins 2012} (stroke, age 35+): HS $\sim$\textbf{+2.7\%/$^\circ$C} (lags 0--4);
        IS $\sim$\textbf{+1.6\%/$^\circ$C} below $\sim$22$^\circ$C (lags 0--13).
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Night heat: intensity $\neq$ binary flag.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Guo 2024}: official HN (Tmin\,$\geq$\,28$^\circ$C) \textbf{null} for excess hosp.;
        hot-night \textbf{intensity} $\sim$\textbf{+3.1\%} (circulatory sensitive).
  \item \textbf{Wang 2019}: $\geq$5 consecutive HNs and 2D3N combinations raise mortality.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Modifiers \& warm-climate winter excess.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Guo 2025}: temp.--hosp.\ risks amplified on polluted days.
  \item \textbf{Yang/Chong 2025}: cold + influenza $\uparrow$ stroke ($\sim$1.17M; 22y).
  \item \textbf{Lam 2018}: stronger cold--AMI in diabetes; \textbf{Lorking 2020}: tropical winter stroke excess.
\end{itemize}
