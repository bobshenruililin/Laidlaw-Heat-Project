## Core takeaways

1. **Cold remains co-primary.** Goggins AMI (2013) and stroke (2012) establish cold-dominant historical Hong Kong CVD patterns; subtypes must stay separate.
2. **Official hot nights are contested as biology.** Guo (2024) found binary HNday28 not associated with excess hospitalization risk, while hot-night excess intensity was—including circulatory admissions.
3. **Spell structure matters.** Wang (2019) shows 5+ consecutive HNs/VHDs and 2D3N-type combinations raise mortality risk; monthly totals should be paired with spell metrics.
4. **Pollution can modify temperature effects.** Guo (2025) supports staged models and cautious ozone handling.
5. **Cold + influenza co-travel.** Yang/Chong (2025) weekly stroke analysis supports flu sensitivity and absolute humidity over RH alone.
6. **Monthly ≠ daily.** Bhaskaran/Gasparrini methods imply our design estimates hospital burden, not daily triggering; coefficients are not comparable to Goggins per-°C RRs.

## Defensible novelty statement

Among Hong Kong residents aged 35+, estimate monthly associations of officially defined thermal extremes (especially hot nights and cold days) with principal-diagnosis inpatient AMI, ischemic stroke, and hemorrhagic stroke during 2013–2023, with age–sex offsets, COVID handling, and staged pollution adjustment—without assuming heat replaced cold, and without claiming primacy over recent daily hot-night hospitalization research.
