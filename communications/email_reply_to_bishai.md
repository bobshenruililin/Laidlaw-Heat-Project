**Where things stand, briefly**

- I have processed real HKO data for 2013–2023 into official extreme metrics (hot nights, very hot days, cold days). Descriptively, hot nights and very hot days are clearly higher in 2019–2023 than earlier in the decade; cold days are still present but variable. That is climate description only — not admission results.
- I have a reproducible R workflow ready for Negative Binomial models with age–sex population offsets, seasonality/COVID controls, and separate AMI / ischemic / hemorrhagic stroke strata.
- What we still need before real results: the HA aggregate extract (via Roro), C&SD denominators, and EPD pollution files (Hogan). I have not estimated any real temperature–admission associations yet; synthetic runs were only to test the code.
