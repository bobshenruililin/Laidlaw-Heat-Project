## Jasmine + Roro spine

**Jasmine is confirmed:** Jingwen Liu et al. (2020), *Sustainable Cities and Society* 57:102131, DOI `10.1016/j.scs.2020.102131`. The paper studies Hong Kong daily mortality in 2006–2016 with a DLNM and quasi-Poisson model. Its reported attributable fractions were 4.72% for cold and 0.16% for heat. Those are mortality attributable fractions, not stroke coefficients.

**Roro's baseline:** Zhenyuan Liu et al. (2026), medRxiv DOI `10.64898/2026.03.05.26347683`, estimates model-based excess heat deaths in 2014–2023 using local RRs from Jasmine and Wang/Ren under four definitions: `HWD_Tavg`, `HWD_Tmax`, `HWD_Tmin`, and `HWD_Tcombined`. This establishes a strong multi-definition mortality baseline; it does not supply coefficients for stroke.

**Our estimand:** associations between monthly thermal exposures and Roro's true-event-month stroke aggregates, 2013–2023. Roro owns the outcome timing rule: first GOPC mention is a marker, and the true event month must be recovered. The family is cumulative:

> Jasmine daily mortality → Roro multi-definition excess heat mortality → paired monthly hot/cold stroke morbidity.
