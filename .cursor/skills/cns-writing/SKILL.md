# CNS writing mode

## When to activate

Activate for manuscript-facing prose, Stage 3 essays, abstracts, Methods, Results, Discussion, titles, and substantive revisions when Bob asks for “CNS register,” “publishable quality,” “mature prose,” or a final write-up. Use the canonical essay at [`reports/laidlaw_stage3/essay_lit_methods.md`](../../../reports/laidlaw_stage3/essay_lit_methods.md) as the local style exemplar and [`analysis_plan/writing_standards_hogan.md`](../../../analysis_plan/writing_standards_hogan.md) as the governing voice standard.

Do not use this mode to make incomplete science sound complete. Confirm the estimand, provenance, human decisions, and available results before polishing.

## Non-negotiable voice rules

1. Write spare, plain, digestible academic English.
2. Put one substantive claim in each sentence whenever possible.
3. Prefer concrete estimands, methods, effect sizes, confidence intervals, exposure counts, and limitations to rhetorical summaries.
4. Remove every sentence that adds ceremony but no information.
5. Use formal author–date or numbered citations consistently. Cite papers by author in prose; never substitute internal nicknames for formal attribution.
6. Keep daily mortality attributable fractions, modelled excess deaths, and monthly stroke associations distinct.
7. Credit collaborators by scientific role in the body when their contribution affects the method: for example, “the weather co-investigator” or “the outcome co-investigator.” Reserve personal thanks for acknowledgements. Use full names only when available and appropriate.
8. State planned procedures directly. State completed procedures in the past tense. Report Results only from verified `REAL` or `HA_APPROVED_AGGREGATE` outputs.
9. Describe associations as associations unless the design supports a stronger claim.
10. Preserve uncertainty without performing uncertainty theatre: name the precise missing source, decision, or limitation once.

## Forbidden phrases and anti-patterns

- Draft markers in scientific prose: “TBD,” “placeholder,” “results forthcoming,” “insert result,” “to be confirmed,” or “work in progress.”
- Internal process language in the scientific body: “Gate 3,” “pipeline,” “Tuesday lock,” “Laidlaw admin,” “our Jasmine paper,” or “Roro’s paper.”
- Empty emphasis: “very interesting,” “remarkably,” “groundbreaking,” “novel and important,” “it is important to note,” or “needless to say.”
- Significance-count narratives, p-value shopping, or presenting a multi-definition panel as independent discoveries.
- Stacked metaphors, scene-setting that delays the estimand, rhetorical questions, and self-congratulatory claims.
- Causal verbs such as “caused,” “proved,” or “demonstrated” for ecological monthly associations.
- Invented values, schema details, subgroup availability, source parameters, or collaborator decisions.
- Synthetic coefficients, exposure-only patterns, mortality AFs, or excess-death estimates presented as stroke findings.

## Checklist before declaring done

- [ ] The opening states the scientific problem and estimand without throat-clearing.
- [ ] Each sentence carries one clear claim; redundant clauses and paragraphs are cut.
- [ ] Every quantitative statement is checked against a source or verified real output.
- [ ] Citations are formal, complete, and attached to the claims they support.
- [ ] Collaborator contributions are credited by role in the body and appropriately in acknowledgements.
- [ ] No draft markers, process gossip, internal gate language, or administrative logistics remain in the scientific body.
- [ ] Results distinguish effect estimates, confidence intervals, exposure prevalence, missingness, and diagnostics.
- [ ] Null, discordant, and sensitivity estimates are not hidden.
- [ ] Daily mortality, modelled excess mortality, and monthly stroke morbidity are not conflated.
- [ ] Limitations match the monthly ecological design and governed aggregate data.
- [ ] No claim exceeds the provenance of the underlying data.
- [ ] The final read matches the register of [`reports/laidlaw_stage3/essay_lit_methods.md`](../../../reports/laidlaw_stage3/essay_lit_methods.md) and the rules in [`analysis_plan/writing_standards_hogan.md`](../../../analysis_plan/writing_standards_hogan.md).
